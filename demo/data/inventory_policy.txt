ACME GLOBAL SUPPLY CHAIN — INVENTORY MANAGEMENT POLICY
Version 3.2 | Effective: January 1, 2025 | Owner: VP Supply Chain Operations
Document Type: inventory_policy

════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 1 — PURPOSE & SCOPE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This policy governs inventory management across all ACME distribution centres and
production facilities globally. It applies to all finished goods, raw materials,
work-in-progress (WIP), and packaging materials.

Non-compliance with reorder triggers carries a financial penalty: stockout events
are budgeted at $450 per lost unit sale equivalent (fully-loaded opportunity cost).
Excess inventory carrying cost is budgeted at 22% per annum of inventory value.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 2 — SAFETY STOCK FORMULA
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Safety Stock (SS) is calculated using the statistical formula:

    SS = Z × σ_demand × √(Lead_time)

Where:
    Z             = service level factor (see table below)
    σ_demand      = standard deviation of daily demand (last 90 days)
    Lead_time     = supplier lead time in days

Service Level Targets by Category:
    Consumer Electronics  → 97.5% (Z = 1.96)
    Industrial Automation → 99.0% (Z = 2.33) — due to high stockout cost
    Raw Materials         → 95.0% (Z = 1.65)
    Packaging Materials   → 95.0% (Z = 1.65)

The safety stock formula is reviewed quarterly by the Planning team and updated
based on observed demand variability and service level performance.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 3 — REORDER POINT POLICY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Reorder Point (ROP) formula:
    ROP = (Average Daily Demand × Lead Time in Days) + Safety Stock

Systems integration: ROP is configured in the ERP system (SAP S/4HANA) and
triggers automatic purchase requisitions when on-hand + on-order inventory
drops below the ROP threshold.

Manual override: Any manual override of an ERP-generated purchase requisition
requires approval from the Category Manager and documentation in the deviation log.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 4 — ECONOMIC ORDER QUANTITY (EOQ)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The EOQ model is applied to all replenishable SKUs:

    EOQ = √(2 × D × S / H)

Where:
    D = annual demand (units)
    S = ordering cost per purchase order ($85 standard; $250 for international)
    H = holding cost per unit per year (22% of unit cost)

Minimum order quantities (MOQs) from suppliers may override EOQ.
When MOQ > EOQ, procurement places MOQ and documents the variance.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 5 — WEEKS OF SUPPLY (WoS) TARGETS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

    Category                  Min WoS   Target WoS   Max WoS
    ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    Consumer Electronics          4          6            8
    Industrial Automation         6          8           12
    Raw Materials                 3          4            6
    Packaging Materials           2          3            5
    MRO / Spare Parts             4         10           24   (irregular demand)

WoS is calculated as: On-Hand Inventory / Weekly Demand Rate (13-week rolling avg).
Items outside the Min/Max corridor trigger a mandatory review by the S&OP team.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 6 — OBSOLESCENCE POLICY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Slow-moving inventory triggers:
  - Yellow flag : 90 days with no movement → category manager notified
  - Red flag    : 180 days with no movement → write-down review initiated
  - Write-off   : 365 days with no movement → CFO approval required, 100% write-down

End-of-life (EOL) inventory process:
  1. Product EOL declared by Product Management
  2. Final buy quantity calculated (last-time-buy analysis)
  3. Excess inventory disposition options assessed: resale, scrap, donation
  4. Decision documented and approved by VP Supply Chain

════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 7 — CYCLE COUNT & AUDIT POLICY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Cycle count frequency:
  - A-items (top 20% by value): monthly count
  - B-items (next 30% by value): quarterly count
  - C-items (remaining 50%): annual count

Annual full physical inventory audit: Q4 (October), conducted by independent
third-party audit firm. Inventory accuracy target: ≥ 99.5% by value.

Inventory accuracy is defined as:
    (Units counted / Units per ERP record) × 100

All discrepancies ≥ $500 or ≥ 5% on a SKU must be investigated and root-caused
within 5 business days of identification.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 8 — JUST-IN-TIME (JIT) ITEMS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Perishable and high-value items designated as JIT:
  - No safety stock held; consumption-synchronized delivery
  - Supplier must maintain a consignment stock buffer of 3 days demand
  - JIT items are reviewed for reclassification semi-annually
  - Current JIT items: SKU-CE-1002 (Wireless Earbuds Pro) at Memphis DC only

════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 9 — EXCEPTION APPROVALS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Approval thresholds for policy exceptions:
  - Safety stock below formula: Category Manager approval
  - WoS above Max corridor: VP Supply Chain + CFO approval
  - EOQ override > 20%: Category Manager + Finance approval
  - New supplier stocking (first order): Supplier Dev + Legal review

All exceptions must be logged in the Supply Chain Policy Deviation Register
and reviewed at the monthly S&OP meeting.
