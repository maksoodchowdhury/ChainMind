SECTION 1 — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Total forecasted demand for Q1 2025 is 1.84 million units across all product categories,
representing a 12% increase over Q1 2024. Key drivers include new product launches in
the Industrial Automation segment and seasonal uplift in Consumer Electronics.

Risk-adjusted forecast (P80 confidence): 1.92 million units.

SECTION 5 — DEMAND PLANNING ASSUMPTIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. No major macroeconomic disruptions assumed in Q1 2025.
2. Valentine's Day promotion (Feb 10–14) expected to lift Consumer Electronics by 22%.
3. Q1 seasonal dip in Latin America (summer holidays) already factored in.
4. Raw material price increases are passed through to finished goods pricing with 30-day lag.
5. All lead times assume no port congestion. Port congestion risk: MEDIUM for Q1 (see Section 5 of Risk Report).
6. Currency: all volumes in USD. EUR/USD assumed at 1.09 for European cost calculations.
